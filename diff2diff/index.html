Abstract

AI can help make music education more accessible by simplifying complex piano pieces. However, most current methods use proprietary datasets and MIDI format, which limits their practical use and prevents researchers from building upon existing work.

We present a transformer-based method for simplifying MusicXML piano scores. Instead of using manually annotated data, we create a synthetic dataset with pairs of original and simplified versions of the same piece. Our method uses pretrained models to ensure appropriate difficulty levels and maintain musical style.

Experiments show our method accurately controls difficulty while preserving musical quality. We openly release all code, data, and models to promote reproducible research and support inclusive music education.
