About

Technology and music are my passions. It is my primary motivation to pursue research in sound and music computing and music information retrieval. I have previously researched on Harmony-related topics, and I am currently pursuing my PhD studies on the intersection between music performance and artificial intelligence at Music Technology Group , Universitat Pompeu Fabra, Barcelona.

My PhD thesis, "Can I play it? Understanding piano performance difficulty through explainable and multimodal machine learning" focuses on audio and symbolic music dataset creation and deep learning for performance difficulty analysis under the supervision of Prof. Xavier Serra . We aim to understand the performance difficulty, through pedagogically motivated representations, to recommend a customised and optimised learning path for each music student and allow musicians explore large music score collections.

In the final phase of my PhD, I completed two research internships in music generation: at Sony CSL, Tokyo (summer 2023) and at Yamaha R\&D. At Sony CSL I worked on inpainting as a "copilot" for composers. At Yamaha R\&D I adapted Llama to generate personalised practice exercises for students. These projects deepened my knowledge of music generation and led to ongoing work with my colleagues, Dasaem Jeong and Emilia Parada-Cabaleiro, on generative systems for music education.

I also work on representation learning for audio with large models and large-scale data. The aim is to build models with broad "music understanding" that support many tasks, including tagging, transcription, generation, and recommendation. I develop this line with my colleagues at the Music Technology Group at Essentia Labs.
